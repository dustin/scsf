Who Are We?

The Santa Clara Schools Foundation is a non-profit, public benefit corporation formed in 1992 to fund innovative projects and programs proposed by teachers for the Santa Clara Unified School District.

The foundation's fund-raising focus is on private sources from local corporations , to small businesses, community friends and Santa Clara Unified Alumni.

Since its inception, this all volunteer organization has funded over $450,000 of programs for our district schools.

Why Do We Do This?

In the last three years, Santa Clara Unified Schools have suffered more than $13 million in cuts. For the 2005-2006 school year, the district's budget will be $3.4 million less than it was last year. This is the equivalent of $250 less per student.

This budget shortfall is primarily a result of state funding cutbacks coupled with a continued decline in local commercial property tax revenues. This year's cuts include reducing Kindergarten from a full day to half day, decreasing the number of nurses and counselors in our schools.

Other programs that were on the cut list but managed to survive (this year) were the elementary school music program, library assistance, maintaining small class sizes for third and ninth graders, and many more.
