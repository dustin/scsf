Who Are We?

The Santa Clara Schools Foundation is a non-profit, all-volunteer, public benefit corporation formed in 1992 to fund projects and programs for the Santa Clara Unified School District. The Foundation is not supported by taxpayer dollars.

Over the past 13 years, the Foundation has raised money through donations and fundraisers. The board of directors has received, reviewed, and funded grant requests for classroom projects and supplies from teachers throughout our district. Since its inception, the Foundation has provided over $450,000 to our district schools in the form of these grants.

Due to the recent budget crisis, in addition to continuing the grant program, the Foundation has decided increase its efforts to support the schools by launching an annual campaign.

What About the Parent Teacher Association (PTA)?

Our PTAs work very hard to serve the needs of their respective schools. The PTAs raise money for many of the important extras that the schools would not otherwise be able to supply.

What they can't do (by charter) is raise money for teacher salaries or build an endowment. The Foundation is raising funds for the entire district.

Funds raised will be used to support instructional programs that would have otherwise been cut. We urge you to support both your PTA and the Foundation.
